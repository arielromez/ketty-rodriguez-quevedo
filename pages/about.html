Ketty Rodriguez Quevedo

Ketty Rodríguez Quevedo is a Cuban visual artist whose work navigates poetic symbolism through painting. She began her artistic training at the prestigious San Alejandro National Academy of Fine Arts— one of the oldest and most respected art schools in the Americas—and later graduated with honors from the University of the Arts (ISA) in Havana, an elite institution that selects only a handful of students nationwide each year.

Her artistic journey includes numerous solo and group exhibitions in Cuba, the United States, and Europe. In 2016, she was selected for a residency at the Vermont Studio Center, a competitive program that supports emerging voices in contemporary art. That same year, she began exhibiting in the U.S., expanding her practice across borders. During her final year at ISA, American artist Frank Stella visited her studio and offered an impromptu talk on her work, recognizing its strength.
